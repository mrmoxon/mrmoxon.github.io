some important things i believe:

  • on the advancement of civilisation
    • universe is hyper young, life on Earth an outlier
    • sustained flourishing of life is the moral duty of any (our) intelligence
    • democratising intelligence will supercharge abundance and quality of life
    • transformers provide a stable and scalable paradigm for early AGI by '28
  • the alignment problem defines the core behaviour of any society
    • it is the triple point of "paleolithic emotions, medieval institutions and godlike technology" - e.o.w.
    • in other words, we strike a balance between not dying and not ossifying
  • lessons from economics:
    • EMH is weak at best, all opportunities emerge in the gaps of market rationality
    • the fate of billions is steered overwhelmingly by the work of a few
    • consolidations of market power are dangerous and wasteful, but maximise investor returns
  • lessons from research:
    • ultimate promise of LLMs is agents, ultimate promise of agents is autoscience
    • gpt to agent(s) updates prompting from "write me an essay" to "make me a dyson syphere"
    • digital platforms proliferate hyperstimuli, both actively and automatically. reels/tiktok are 0.5-d wireheading
    • with LLMs we tread closer to true natural abstraction - next stop: thought-to-x
  • some other thoughts:
    • innovation and agility thrive in small, focused groups working fast
    • progress hinges on our willingness to ask more meaningful questions. there is no fix-all, only convergence
    • such courage is authentic when it comes with a genuine excitement for the work itself
